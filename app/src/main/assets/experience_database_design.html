DATABASE DESIGN

Real world experience

After so many years working with hundreds of databases, I've learned so much about what works and what does not.

When accessing and modifying data, often I have to worry about many different aspects because the database designer had done little or no planning, which in turn makes things more challenging than they have to be.

At that point, I began digging into database design, so I could suggest appropriate optimizations, so the system runs more efficiently, sometimes avoiding unnecessary hardware upgrades.

Right out of college in 1999, I didn't really care much about proper database design, however, as I was exposed to many database engines and had to develop systems that used all kinds of designs.

Knowing the ups and downs of so many approaches gives me the edge to work under a great database design or under less than ideal circumstances.
