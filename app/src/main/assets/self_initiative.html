SELF INITIATIVE

Professionally

Going all the way back to high school, I've realized that whenever I did only the bare minimum of a certain task, like homework or projects, my teachers would simply nod and say a very boring "good job".

However, if I went above and beyond and did my personal best, they would say a slow and well toned "well done!".

Of course, I always wanted the earn the highest compliment, so it became a bit of my personal challenge to always give my best in whatever I do.

Needless to say, college was a blast and so has been my professional life.

Personally

I've realized that you cannot be someone at home and someone completely different at work. At one point a lot of those traits will cross over to one side or the other.

My self-initiative starts at home, where I dedicate as much as possible to spending time with my family, progressing, learning, becoming a better human being.

My personal goal is to keep yourself going, because idleness only encourages even more idleness.

Another factor is becoming a parent, where my children pay very close attention to the things I do or say, so I try even harder to be a good example to my entire family, so they know what a life of honest hard work is worth.
